Antiderivative:
(1) A function F is an antiderivative of a function f if F is differentiable and F' = f

Derivative:
(1) The derivative of a function f(x) is a function f'(x) such that...

f'(x) = lim h->0 (f(x + h) - f(x)) / h

at every point x in the domain of f(x) where the limit is defined. (Source: Art of Problem Solving)

Combinatorics:
(1) The field of mathematics concerned with counting

Definite integral:
(1) The definite integral of a continuous function f(x) on a closed interval [a, b] is the Riemann sum approximating the area under the curve as the number of rectangles approaches infinity.

Function:
(1) A rule assigning to every input exactly one output
(2) A rule that assigns to every input exactly one output

Fundamental theorem of algebra:
(1) For any polynomial P(x) of degree n the following two statements are true: P(x) = 0 has exactly n complex solutions, and P(x) can be written as a(x - r1)(x - r2)...(x - rn) where a is the leading coefficient of P(x) and r1, r2, ..., rn is a sequence of n complex numbers, which are called the "roots" of the polynomial.

Fundamental theorem of arithmetic:
(1) The fundamental theorem of arithmetic states that every positive integer greater than one has a unique prime factorization

Fundamental theorem of calculus:
(1) The definite integral of a continuous function f(x) on the closed interval [a, b] is F(b) - F(a), where F is any antiderivative of f(x).

Limit:
(1) Let f(x) be a real-valued function. The limit of f(x) as x approaches a is L, or lim x->a f(x) = L, if, for all e > 0, there exists a d > 0 such that 0 < |x - a| < d implies |f(x) - L| < e. (Source: Art of Problem Solving)

Secant:
(1) A secant line to the graph of y = f(x) is any line that intersects the graph in at least two distinct points. (Source: Art of Problem Solving)
